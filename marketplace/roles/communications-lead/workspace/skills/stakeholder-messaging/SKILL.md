# Stakeholder Messaging

Guidelines for crafting audience-appropriate communications across the company's key stakeholder groups.

## Audience Profiles

### Customers
- **Priority**: Trust, transparency, practical impact
- **Tone**: Warm, clear, solution-focused
- **Channels**: Email, in-app, status page, social media
- **Avoid**: Jargon, overpromising, delayed acknowledgement

### Investors
- **Priority**: Financial impact, strategic context, management confidence
- **Tone**: Professional, data-driven, forward-looking
- **Channels**: Investor updates, earnings calls, direct emails
- **Avoid**: Speculation, minimising material risks, inconsistency with public disclosures

### Partners
- **Priority**: Operational impact, joint response, relationship continuity
- **Tone**: Collaborative, specific, solution-oriented
- **Channels**: Direct contact (email/call), partner portal
- **Avoid**: One-size-fits-all messaging; partners need tailored context

### Regulators
- **Priority**: Compliance, factual accuracy, timeliness
- **Tone**: Formal, precise, cooperative
- **Channels**: Official correspondence, designated regulatory contact
- **Avoid**: Informality, speculation, delayed notification

### Employees
- **Priority**: Clarity, honesty, what it means for them
- **Tone**: Direct, reassuring, action-oriented
- **Channels**: All-hands, Slack/Teams, manager cascade
- **Avoid**: Corporate spin, information vacuum, leaving rumours unfilled

## Message Architecture

Every stakeholder communication should have three layers:

1. **Headline** — The single most important thing they need to know (one sentence)
2. **Context** — Why it matters to them specifically (2–3 sentences)
3. **Action** — What they should do, or what we are doing on their behalf (bullet points)

## Consistency Check

Before sending, verify:
- [ ] Factually consistent with all other active communications
- [ ] Approved by Legal if it touches liability, regulation, or litigation
- [ ] CEO/relevant executive aware and aligned
- [ ] Timing coordinated (don't let different audiences hear conflicting things simultaneously)
- [ ] Tone matches the audience profile above

## Version Control

Maintain a communications log in `memory/MEMORY.md`:
- Date and time of each communication
- Audience
- Key messages
- Approvers
- Outcome / response received
