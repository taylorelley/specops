# Crisis Communications

Framework for managing company communications before, during, and after a crisis event.

## Crisis Severity Levels

| Level | Description | Response Time | Examples |
|-------|-------------|---------------|---------|
| **Critical** | Major reputational or legal risk | < 30 minutes | Data breach, executive misconduct, service outage affecting customers |
| **High** | Significant negative press or customer impact | < 2 hours | Product failure, partner dispute, regulatory inquiry |
| **Medium** | Limited exposure, contained issue | < 4 hours | Minor service disruption, social media criticism |
| **Low** | Isolated, low-visibility incident | < 1 business day | Individual customer complaint, minor factual error in coverage |

## First 30 Minutes Checklist

- [ ] **Assess** — Confirm facts, scope, and which stakeholders are affected
- [ ] **Align** — Brief CEO and Legal Counsel before any external statement
- [ ] **Draft holding statement** — Acknowledge the situation; avoid speculation
- [ ] **Lock social channels** — Pause scheduled posts; monitor for mentions
- [ ] **Assign spokesperson** — Designate a single point of external contact
- [ ] **Set update cadence** — Commit to regular updates (every 30–60 min for Critical)

## Statement Templates

### Holding Statement (immediate)

```
We are aware of [issue] and are actively investigating.
The safety and trust of our customers is our top priority.
We will provide an update by [time].
```

### Incident Acknowledgement

```
[Company] experienced [brief description of issue] beginning at [time].
We understand the impact this has had on [affected parties] and sincerely apologise.

What happened: [1–2 sentences, factual only]
What we did: [actions taken]
Current status: [resolved / ongoing / monitoring]

We will share a full update within [timeframe].
```

### Resolution Statement

```
We have resolved the [issue] that affected [scope] from [start time] to [end time].

Root cause: [brief, non-technical explanation]
Steps taken: [key remediation actions]
Prevention: [what we are doing to prevent recurrence]

We apologise for the disruption and thank our customers for their patience.
For further questions: [contact / link]
```

## Internal Alert Template

```
🚨 [LEVEL] Crisis Alert — [Title]

Summary: [1–2 sentences]
Affected: [Products / customers / regions]
Status: [Investigating / Mitigating / Resolved]
External statement: [Issued / Pending / Not required]
Spokesperson: [Name]
Next update: [Time]

Do NOT speak to press or post publicly. Direct all enquiries to [name/contact].
```

## Media Relations Guidelines

- **Never speculate** — Only confirm what is known; say "we are investigating" for unknowns
- **One voice** — All external statements route through Communications Lead
- **Background vs on-record** — Agree with Legal before going on background
- **Correction protocol** — Issue corrections promptly; never let inaccurate coverage stand
- **Embargo** — Use for positive news only; never embargo negative news

## Post-Crisis Review

Within 48 hours of resolution:

1. **Timeline audit** — Were response times met? Where were delays?
2. **Message audit** — Were statements accurate, consistent, and timely?
3. **Media coverage review** — Sentiment, reach, key narratives
4. **Stakeholder feedback** — Customer, partner, and investor reactions
5. **Lessons learned** — What to improve in the communications runbook

## Escalation Path

1. Communications Lead — owns all external messaging
2. Legal Counsel — review required for all public statements during Critical/High events
3. CEO — final approval on Critical-level statements
4. Board — notification required for material events (regulatory obligations may apply)
