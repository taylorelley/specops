# Stakeholder Communications

Recurring communication with the people who need ongoing visibility into the company — without overwhelming them.

## Cadence by Audience

| Audience | Cadence | Format | Length |
|----------|---------|--------|--------|
| Whole company | Weekly | Internal note / all-hands recap | 300–500 words |
| Exec team | Weekly | Briefing doc | 1 page |
| Board | Monthly | Board update | 2–3 pages |
| Investors | Monthly | Investor letter | 1–2 pages |
| Strategic partners | Quarterly | Partner update | 1 page |

## Weekly All-Hands Note

```
# Week of {date}

## Wins
- {win 1}
- {win 2}

## Lowlights / What we learned
- {issue 1 — what we're doing about it}

## Numbers
- {metric}: {value} ({delta vs last week})

## What's next
- {focus for next week}

## Shout-outs
- {team / individual}
```

```
## Wins
{narrative}

{narrative — what's hard, what we're trying}

```

## Exec Briefing Template

```
# Briefing: {Topic} — {date}

**Decision needed**: {yes/no — by when}
**Audience**: {who}

## TL;DR
{2–3 sentences}

## Background
{context, 1 paragraph}

## Options
1. {option} — pros / cons / cost
2. {option} — pros / cons / cost

## Recommendation
{one option, with rationale}

## Risks
- {risk + mitigation}
```

## Tone Guidelines

- **Internal staff** — Direct, candid, includes lowlights. Trust grows with honesty.
- **Investors** — Data-forward, framed by trajectory, asks are concrete.
- **Board** — Strategic and decision-oriented; preserve their time.
- **Partners** — Outcome-focused; what they unlock for the partnership.
- **Customers** — Benefit-led, plain language, no internal jargon.

## Tracking Confirmation & Feedback

- Log every recurring update in MEMORY.md with date, audience, and link.
- For board / investor updates, track read receipts or follow-up replies — silence is a signal.
- Capture specific asks raised by stakeholders and route them to owners with a due date.
- Quarterly: review whether each cadence still earns its place. Cancel updates no one acts on.
